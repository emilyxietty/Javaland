About

Javaland is a small island off the coast of Philippines. During the COVID-19 pandemic of 2021, Javalandâ€™s founder, Emily Xie, decided to travel the world in her trusty yacht. Unknown to her, she would discover the now-famous land of Javaland. 30 years since its founding, Javaland has become become well known for its agriculture, fishing, and tourism industries. Similar to other coastal Asian countries, Javaland is known for its proximity to the ocean, mountainous terrain, and tropical climate. A unique perk of Javaland is its quick modernization - being a newly discovered country, investors poured money into the land to develop its transportation and industry infrastructure.

3 MAIN INDUSTRIES

The three main industries of Javaland are:

    Fishing
    Tourism
    Agriculture

Common fish that are caught are salmon, tuna, and puffer fish. These fish are high quality and very healthy for consumption, as human pollution has affected this region very little.

3 MAIN EXPORTS

The three main exports of Javaland are:

    Seafood
    Rice
    Bananas

Being a tropical, coastal climate, bananas, rice and seafood are abundant.

TRANSPORTATION

The three main forms of transport in Javaland are:

    Speedrail
    Zipline
    Bicycle

As this is a region with many mountains, many locals resort to using ziplines to commute, as it is quick and an enjoyable start to the day! However, speedrails are also available for those in a hurry.

Culture

Javaland's culture is a mixed bag of cultures from other countries. As it was completely uninhabited until 2021, this new land became a prime opportunity for civilians in nearby countries. Families from the Philippines, Vietnam, Hong Kong, Japan, and more moved to Javaland for the opportunities it provided. Thus began the mixing pot of multiculturalism, along with its incorporation of the land's resources.

DISHES

The three main dishes in Javaland are:

    Banana Sticky Rice
    Sushi
    Banana Bread

Banana Sticky Rice, a derivation of the classic "Mango Sticky Rice", incorporates prime parts of the Javaland industry into its food. Popularized by Thai immigrants to Javaland, this food has become a staple among the locals. A banana bread variation made of rice flour has also become a staple amongst the locals.

PASSTIMES

The three main passtimes in Javaland are:

    Scuba diving
    Ziplining
    Hiking

Javaland has large mountains with safe trails for tourists to roam. Additionally, it has become a popular scuba diving location, and has an intricate underwater cave system to explore. Ocean conservation committees work closely with locals and our authorities to preserve this land, as Javaland has had the unique benefit of being untouched throughout the mass industrialization of humankind.
